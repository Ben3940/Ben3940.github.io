Sentiment Analysis AI

This AI is given a review of a park and predicts if the review is positive or negative, given the words used in the sentence. This AI uses naive Bayes' theorem as its model. To train the model, it was given 2000 positive review and 2000 negative reviews. It then tokenized each word from every positive sentence and determined the probability of a word being associated with positivity. For example, if the word "good" appeared more often than "ok" in the positive reivews, then "good" will lead to a higher probability of a sentence being positive, given that the word "good" is in it. The AI will tokenize the negative reviews the same way. At the end, the AI will ask if its prediction was correct, giving you the chance to teach it and help it improve its model.

Tic Tac Toe AI

This AI will play tic tac toe with you. It will always tie or beat you in every game, it can never lose. It uses a Minimax algroithm to predict every move that can be made. By doing so, the AI can evaluate which set of moves will direct it towards the optimal outcome. For tic tac toe, there are not that many moves to consider, however, the AI takes much longer to make its first move than it does it second to last, because there are less moves it has to consider.

Electronics

Though anyone can program without worrying about what is going on inside their computer, it is still quite benefical to understand electronics to some extent. Arduino and Raspberry Pi make working with electronics user-friendly and greatly accelerate the development process.

Simple Calculator

This basic calculator can add, subtract, divide, and multiply numbers. It uses a ATmega2560 Arduino board with a LCD to show input/output to/from the microcontroller. The A, B, C, and D, keys on the keypad were programmed as the add, subtract, multiply, and divide buttons, respectivly. The pound key does nothing and the asterisk button replicates the multiply button's functionality (this is the default behavior of the asterisk key). There is even a potentiometer to adjust the brightness of the LCD.

Boat Under the Moonlight

This scene does not set out to be over complicated or involve too much. I created a simple, low-poly model of a boat and, placed it out on a plane with a reflective dark-blue material. The white spheres in the sky emit their own light, which is how they create reflections on the water. This image was rendered with Blender's Cycles rendering engine.
